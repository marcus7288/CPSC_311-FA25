üîÑ The Systems Development Life Cycle (SDLC)

Systems Development Life Cycle (SDLC)

A structured approach to developing information systems that guides project teams through a series of predefined phases, each with specific activities, deliverables, and milestones.

1. Planning

Identify system needs, assess feasibility, and develop project plans

2. Analysis

Gather requirements, understand current systems, and define system specifications

3. Design

Create system architecture, user interfaces, and detailed technical specifications

4. Implementation

Build, test, and deploy the system to production environment

5. Maintenance

Support, enhance, and adapt the system throughout its operational life

Methodology Comparison Workshop (30 minutes)

Traditional vs. Agile Development

Aspect Traditional (Waterfall) Agile
Planning Approach Extensive upfront planning Adaptive planning throughout
Requirements Fixed early in project Evolve based on feedback
Development Cycles Single, sequential cycle Multiple, iterative cycles
Customer Involvement Limited to specific phases Continuous throughout project
Change Management Difficult and expensive Expected and accommodated

Discussion Questions:

  • When might waterfall be more appropriate than agile?
  • What are the risks of each approach?
  • How do team size and project complexity affect methodology choice?

Assignment 1.2: Methodology Selection Case Study

Due: End of Week 1 (Friday, 11:59 PM)

Points: 30

You've been hired as a consultant to help Pine Valley Furniture Company select an appropriate development methodology for their new e-commerce system. Analyze the situation and provide a recommendation.

Case Background:
  • Company: Traditional furniture manufacturer expanding online
  • Timeline: 18-month project deadline
  • Budget: $2.5 million with limited flexibility
  • Team: Mix of internal IT staff and external contractors
  • Requirements: Integration with existing systems, mobile-responsive design
  • Stakeholders: Executive team, sales staff, customers, IT department
Required Analysis:
  1. Methodology Evaluation: Compare at least 3 different approaches
  2. Risk Assessment: Identify risks for each methodology
  3. Recommendation: Select and justify your chosen approach
  4. Implementation Plan: High-level timeline and milestones
  5. Success Metrics: How will you measure project success?
Deliverables:
  • 3-4 page written analysis
  • Comparison matrix (can be table or chart)
  • High-level project timeline
  • Professional presentation format
