Opening Discussion (10 minutes)

Share findings from your personal systems inventory. What patterns did you notice? Which systems are most critical to your daily operations?

Concept Exploration: What is a System? (20 minutes)
System

A collection of interrelated components that work together toward a common goal or purpose.

Information System

A combination of hardware, software, data, networks, and people that organizations use to collect, process, store, and disseminate information.

Interactive Exercise: In small groups, identify and analyze the university's course registration system using the characteristics of systems.

Systems Analyst Role Exploration (25 minutes)

Explore the multifaceted role of systems analysts through case study analysis:

  • Technical Skills: Programming, database design, system architecture
  • Analytical Skills: Problem solving, logical thinking, attention to detail
  • Communication Skills: User interviewing, documentation, presentation
  • Management Skills: Project coordination, team leadership, time management
Skills Assessment Tool
Real-World Systems Analysis (20 minutes)

Analyze Evangel University's student information system as a class:

  • What are the system's inputs, processes, and outputs?
  • Who are the stakeholders?
  • What challenges might exist in this system?
  • How might we improve it?

Assignment 1.1: Personal Systems Analysis

Due: Day 2 (before next class)

Points: 25

Building on your pre-class inventory, conduct a detailed analysis of one information system you use regularly. Your analysis should include:

Required Components:
